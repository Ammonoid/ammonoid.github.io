我关注理论、知识与历史。我目前正进行的理论研究可以概括为探求当代社会理论当中已有的诸最简立场（我将立场视作可以锚定特定经验性设问的最简理论组合），并分析支撑这些立场得以运作的认识论基础。我试图说明，鉴于这些认识论基础总是可以表述为源自经验世界的一系列时间性与规范性形式，因而当下的理论发现工作可以从考察不同的时间性与规范性形式及其可能组合入手。具体而言，我识别出两种典范性的社会理论立场，分别是视角主义-位置主义（Perspectivism-Positionalism, P-P）立场以及联结主义-生态主义（Connectionism-Ecologism, C-E）立场。尽管二者并非正交，但鉴于其社会认识论基础建立于不同的时间性与规范性承诺上，的确存在实质性差别。Pierre Bourdieu的“社会神正论”与Robert Brandom的“道义计分”隐喻最显著地体现出P-P立场的实质性承诺；Niklas Luhmann的语义生态学与Harrison White的语用生态学则最突出地呈现了C-E立场的关切。<br><br>
I am interested in theory, knowledge, and history. My current theoretical project can be summarized as an exploration of the existing minimal stances in contemporary social theory (where I define "stances" as the most minimal theoretical combinations capable of anchoring specific empirical inquiries) and an analysis of the epistemological foundations that sustain these positions. I aim to demonstrate that since these epistemological foundations can always be expressed as a series of temporal and normative forms derived from the empirical world, the current work of theoretical discovery can proceed by examining different temporal and normative forms and their possible combinations. Specifically, I identify two canonical social theoretical stances: the Perspectivism-Positionalism (P-P) stance and the Connectionism-Ecologism (C-E) stance. Although the two are not orthogonal, they do exhibit essential differences due to their social epistemological foundations being built upon distinct temporal and normative commitments. Pierre Bourdieu’s "sociodicy" and Robert Brandom’s "deontic scorekeeping" metaphor most prominently embody the essential commitments of the P-P stance, while Niklas Luhmann’s semantic ecology and Harrison White’s pragmatic ecology most vividly represent the concerns of the C-E stance.<br><br>
我的经验与理论锚点在于组织与思想演化问题——事实上，思想与组织总是共同演化。在上述理论工作的基础上，我试图通过一系列由神经科学的学习与记忆研究所启发的理论框架探讨1900-1920年代中国的组织与思想演化（特别是某些思想的衰老与消亡）。具体而言，我尤其关注梁启超的儒家自由主义、章太炎的文化国族主义以及盛行于随后代际的社会主义。我的硕士毕业论文聚焦于其中一个组织涌现时刻（某些共产主义小组如何经由多重力量正式化为中国共产党组织）；我计划在博士期间探讨上述思想如何在整个思想生态当中沉浮兴衰。这项研究关注中国的现代性时刻，并服务于一个关键问题：我们（作为当代中国人）在多大程度上（不）认识自己。这项历史研究并非没有理论私心——我试图在这一过程中挖掘出章太炎作为经典社会理论家的可能，从而为社会理论的寰宇主义提供洞见。<br><br>
My empirical and theoretical anchor lies in the issue of organizational and ideological evolution—in fact, ideology and organization always co-evolve. Building on the aforementioned theoretical work, I attempt to explore the organizational and ideological evolution in China during the 1900s–1920s (particularly the aging and death of certain ideologies) through a series of theoretical frameworks inspired by neuroscience research on learning and memory. Specifically, I focus on Liang Qichao's Confucian liberalism, Zhang Taiyan’s cultural nationalism, and the socialism that prevailed in subsequent generations. My master’s thesis centered on one moment of organizational emergence (how certain communist groups or _xiaozu_ formalized into the Chinese Communist Party through multiple forces); I plan to examine during my doctoral studies how these ideologies rose and fell within the broader ideological ecology. This research focuses on China’s moment of modernity and serves a key question: To what extent do we (as contemporary Chinese people) (fail to) know ourselves? This historical inquiry is not without theoretical ambition—I seek in this process to uncover Zhang Taiyan’s potential as a classical social theorist, thereby offering insights for cosmopolitanism in social theory.<br><br>
鉴于当代社会科学的使命逐渐由描述迈向解释、由单一认识论迈向复数社会认识论，重要的是站在理论与历史的基点上反省何为复数主义。世界范围内社会理论的衰落无疑同时是幸运与不幸：一方面，理论的失焦为社会科学家提供了难得的喘息，鉴于马克思主义、帕森斯主义或布迪厄主义的分散阵营同时被反理论主义取代，理论主义者终于有机会握手言和并商讨这些“主义”的全新可能性；另一方面，由于理论作为立场的穿透力丧失在经验之海，社会科学家同小说家、报社记者、金融分析师与新媒体博主的社会分工日益模糊，社会科学无法基于自身的优势为社会历史过程的解释作出应有的贡献。这就意味着，复数主义同时面临着理论民主化的机遇与令社会科学早衰的风险（我在中性意义上使用这一概念）。我试图通过关于时间性与规范性的讨论趁乱为复数主义做出一些微薄的贡献。<br><br>
Given that the mission of contemporary social science is gradually shifting from description to explanation and from singular epistemology to plural social epistemologies, it is crucial to reflect on what pluralism means from theoretical and historical standpoints. The decline of social theory worldwide is undoubtedly both fortunate and unfortunate: on the one hand, the loss of theoretical focus provides social scientists with a rare respite—as the fragmented camps of Marxism, Parsonianism, or Bourdieusianism are simultaneously replaced by anti-theoretical tendencies, theorists finally have the opportunity to reconcile and explore new possibilities for these "-isms." On the other hand, as theory’s penetrating power as a stance dissipates into the sea of empiricism, the division of labor between social scientists and novelists, journalists, financial analysts, and new media bloggers grows increasingly blurred, leaving social science unable to contribute its due share to the explanation of sociohistorical processes based on its own strengths. This means that pluralism faces both the opportunity of theoretical democratization and the risk of accelerating the premature aging of social science (a term I use neutrally). Amid this chaos, I seek to make a modest contribution to pluralism through discussions on temporality and normativity.
